10 oldest species in the world

Most of the Earth’s history has been uncovered through research into what is known as the fossil record. Because of this research, scientists have been able to piece together the evolutionary tree as well as the evolution of the Earth itself. All of the species on this have ancestors whose fossils date back over hundreds of million years ago. Also, nearly all of these species are known as “living fossils” because they have not evolved too far from their original forms.

Specie Location Age Kindom Earliest Geologic Eon
Horsetails Worldwide except for Antarctica over 300 million Plantae Late Devonian
Coelacanth Coastlines of the Indian Ocean 400 million years Animalia Devonian
Elephant Shark Kaipara Harbour, New Zealand 400 million years Animalia Devonian
Horseshoe Crab Ocean waters and soft sandy 445 million years Animalia Late Ordovician
Nautilus Indo-Pacific seas 500 million years Animalia Late Cambrian
Velvet Worms All tropical habitats 500 million years Animalia Cambrian
Jellyfish Marine waters worldwide 600 – 700 million years Animalia Cambrian
Sponges Marine waters worldwide 600 million years Animalia Precambrian
Ctenophores Marine waters worldwide 700 million years Animalia Cambrian
Cyanobacteria Every terrestrial and aquatic habitat in the world possibly 3.5 billion years Eubacteria Archean
